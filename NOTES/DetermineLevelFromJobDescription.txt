---------------
When looking at a LinkedIn or Indeed job description, what is my algorithm to decide which of the two kinds or roles I am looking at?
---------------

Quick heuristic — scan the job description for these signals:

It's an Architect role if you see emphasis on:

"Design systems," "define architecture," "technical vision/strategy"
"Cross-team," "influence," "stakeholders," "alignment"
"Trade-offs," "scalability," "technical decisions"
"Mentor," "guide," "set standards"
Less emphasis on sprint work, PRs, shipping features directly
It's a Lead/Senior IC role if you see emphasis on:

"Build," "implement," "ship," "deliver"
"Own a component/service/feature end-to-end"
"Code reviews," "pull requests," "hands-on"
"Collaborate with your team," "agile/scrum"
Specific tech stack requirements with depth expectations
The one-line test: Does the job description spend more words on what you'll decide or what you'll build? Decide → Architect. Build → Lead.

In practice, many roles are a blend. When it's ambiguous, look at two tiebreakers:

Title and level — If the posting says "Staff," "Principal," or "Architect," use Software Architect. If it says "Senior," "Lead," or "Sr. Engineer," use Lead Software Engineer.

Team size mentioned — Large team or multiple teams → Architect. Small team or "small, scrappy team" → Lead.

Either way, you don't need to maintain two full resumes — just swap the ZYRLO title line. The bullets work for both.
